If your product contains nanomaterials you need to say if they are:

  • listed in annex 4, 5, or 6 of EC Regulation 1223/2009
  • used in accordance with any restrictions for that material

You need to provide a separate nanomaterial notification if they are not listed or used in accordance with any restrictions.

{% endmacro %} {% macro nanomaterials(headingSize) %}

Nanomaterials

For each nanomaterial the product or item has you need to:

  • provide its IUPAC name
  • state how the user likely to be exposed to the nanomaterial (dermal, oral, or inhalation)
  • state if the user is instructed to rinse off or leave on the product
  • state its purpose (colourant, preservative, UV filter, or other)

If the nanomaterial is not listed in EC regulation 1223/2009, Annex 4, 5, or 6 or used in accordance with the restrictions for that material you need to provide more information.

{% endmacro %} {% macro formulation(headingSize) %}

Formulation

There are 3 ways to submit the formulation of the product:

  • Frame formulation

    A ‘frame formulation’ lists the category or function of ingredients and their maximum concentration in the product. There are frame formulations for most cosmetic products.

    Download the list of frame formulations (PDF)

  • List the ingredients and their concentration range

    For each ingredient give the ingredient and the amount as a range percentage weight for weight (% w/w). For example, aqua above 75%, up to and including 100%.

    How to prepare your ingredients list

  • List the ingredients and their exact concentration

    For each ingredient give the ingredient and the amount as percentage weight for weight (% w/w).

    For example, aqua 85% w/w.

    How to prepare your ingredients list
