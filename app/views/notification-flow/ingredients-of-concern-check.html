Poisonous ingredients

To treat anyone who may be poisoned by the product, National Poison Centres need the exact quantities (% w/w) of the most harmful ingredients.

Glycols and glycol ethers

Above 1%

  • Buteth-2 Acetate
  • Butoxydiglycol
  • Butoxyethanol
  • Butoxyethyl Acetate
  • Diethoxydiglycol
  • Ethoxydiglycol
  • Ethoxydiglycol Acetate
  • Ethylene Glycol Only (INCI name: ‘Glycol’)
  • Glycol Ethers (not Polymers)

Above 10%

  • Dipropylene Glycol
  • Dipropylene Glycol Dimethyl Ether
  • Dipropylene Glycol Isobornyl Ether
  • Methoxyisopropanol
  • Methoxyisopropyl Acetate
  • PPG-2 Methyl Ether
  • PPG-2 Methyl Ether Acetate
  • Propylene Glycol
  • Propylene Glycol Butyl Ether

Hydrocarbon solvents

Above 5%

  • Cyclohexane
  • Hydrogenated Didodecene
  • Isopentane
  • Pentane
  • Toluene
  • Turpentine

Ethanol and isopropanol

Above 1%

  • Benzyl alcohol
  • Furfuryl alcohol
  • n-Butyl alcohol
  • n-Propyl alcohol (INCI name: ‘propyl alcohol’)
  • t-Butyl alcohol

Above 5%

  • Hexyl alcohol

Other ingredients

Any amount of

  • Brucine Sulfate

More than 0.1%

  • Butyrolactone
  • Chloroplatinic Acid
  • Copper Sulfate
  • Dimethyltolylamine
  • Methyl Acetate
  • Potassium Chlorate
  • Sodium Chlorate
  • Triethyl Phosphate
  • m-Cresol, o-Cresol, p-Cresol, Mixed Cresols

More than 0.2%

  • Formaldehyde

More than 1%

  • Benzyl Benzoate
  • Butyl Acetate
  • Ethyl Acetate
  • Hydroxylamine HCL; Hydroxylamine Sulfate

Any ingredients classified as:

  • Alkaline agents
  • Anti-dandruff agents
  • Anti-hair loss agents
  • Anti-pigmenting and depigmenting agents
  • Cationic surfactants
  • Chemical exfoliating agents
  • Compounds releasing hydrogen peroxide
  • Fluoride compounds
  • Hydrogen peroxide
  • Inorganic sodium salts
  • Persulfates
  • Propellants
  • Reducing agents
  • Straightening agents
  • Vitamin A or its derivatives
  • Xanthine derivatives
