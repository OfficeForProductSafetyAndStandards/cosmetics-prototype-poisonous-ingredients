If you sell or give away cosmetic products in the UK, you now need to submit a notification for every product. This covers products already notified to the EU Cosmetic Product Notification Portal (CPNP) before 12 April 2019 (Brexit), as well as products you’ve brought to market since then.

You do not have to submit notifications for cosmetic products taken off the market in the UK before 12 April 2019.

If your products were not notified in the EU before 12 April 2019 (Brexit)

Use the service to give information about:

  • the ‘responsible person’ (this can be an individual or company) who is responsible for ensuring regulations are followed
  • a contact person for people from National Poisons Information Service and Trading Standards to speak to
  • the product’s formulation and whether it contains carcinogenic, mutagenic or toxic for reproduction (CMR) substances, and nanomaterials

You also need to upload an image of the label, as a PDF, JPG or PNG and up to 30 MB in size.

If your product contains certain nanomaterials, you’ll have to wait 6 months before you place the product on the market.

You must submit your notifications for these products before making them available to consumers. If you do not, you may face a penalty of up to £5,000 and a prison term of up to 3 months, or both.

If your products were notified in the EU before Brexit

The process is similar, but you do not need to provide:

  • information about whether or not the cosmetic product contains carcinogenic, mutagenic or toxic for reproduction (CMR) substances
  • an image of the label

You can do this manually or via the ’upload’ method below.

You must submit your notifications for these products by 11 pm GMT on 29 January 2020. If you do not, you may face a penalty of up to £5,000 and a prison term of up to 3 months, or both.
